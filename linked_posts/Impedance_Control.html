Impedance Control

In modern robotics, achieving seamless human-robot interaction is a critical goal for enabling intuitive and effective operation. This project focuses on implementing impedance control for a robotic arm to enhance its compliance and ease of use during manual guidance. By designing an appropriate virtual impedance model, the system can regulate the dynamic interaction between the user and the robotic arm, allowing smooth and effortless dragging motions. The robotic arm used in this project is the Franka Emika Panda, known for its precision and advanced control capabilities. The primary objective is to develop and fine-tune an impedance control framework that minimizes the userâ€™s effort while maintaining stability and responsiveness. The implementation involves careful adjustment of the virtual mass, damping, and stiffness parameters to achieve a balance between compliance and accuracy. By leveraging impedance control, this project aims to improve the human-robot interface, making it suitable for applications requiring physical interaction, such as collaborative tasks, medical assistance, and precision manufacturing. This work demonstrates the potential of impedance-based strategies in advancing robotic systems towards more intuitive and user-friendly designs.
